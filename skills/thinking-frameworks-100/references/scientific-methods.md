# Scientific & Research Methods

Frameworks for rigorous inquiry, evidence evaluation, and knowledge construction.

| #   | Framework                        | Description                                                                                                                                                                  | Origin                          |
| --- | -------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| 86  | **Scientific Method**            | Observation → Question → Hypothesis → Experiment → Analysis → Conclusion. The bedrock of empirical knowledge generation.                                                     | Bacon, Galileo, tradition       |
| 87  | **Hypothetico-Deductive Method** | Form a hypothesis, deduce testable predictions, test them empirically. If predictions fail, revise the hypothesis.                                                           | Karl Popper; logical positivism |
| 88  | **Abductive Reasoning**          | Inference to the best explanation. Given incomplete data, generate the most likely hypothesis — even if not provable. Used in diagnosis, detective work, and design.         | Charles Sanders Peirce          |
| 89  | **Deductive Reasoning**          | Move from general premises to specific conclusions. If the premises are true and the logic valid, the conclusion is necessarily true.                                        | Aristotle                       |
| 90  | **Inductive Reasoning**          | Move from specific observations to general conclusions. Strong induction provides probable (not certain) conclusions. The basis of empirical science.                        | Francis Bacon                   |
| 91  | **Bayesian Updating**            | Start with a prior probability, incorporate new evidence via Bayes' theorem, and arrive at a revised (posterior) probability. The formal engine of rational belief revision. | Thomas Bayes                    |
| 92  | **Fermi Estimation**             | Break an unknown quantity into smaller, estimable parts; multiply/add to get an approximate answer. Develops quantitative intuition and reveals which variables matter most. | Enrico Fermi                    |
| 93  | **Dialectical Method**           | Thesis → Antithesis → Synthesis. Through structured opposition of ideas, arrive at a higher-level understanding that transcends both original positions.                     | Hegel; Socrates                 |
| 94  | **Socratic Method**              | Systematic questioning to expose assumptions, clarify thinking, and guide toward deeper understanding. The teacher asks, the student discovers.                              | Socrates                        |
| 95  | **Red Team / Blue Team**         | Assign a "red team" to attack or critique a plan/hypothesis while a "blue team" defends it. Stress-tests ideas and reveals vulnerabilities.                                  | Military intelligence           |
| 96  | **Delphi Method**                | Aggregate expert judgments through multiple rounds of anonymous forecasting with controlled feedback. Converges toward more accurate predictions.                            | RAND Corporation                |

## Key Sources

- _The Logic of Scientific Discovery_ — Karl Popper
- _Superforecasting_ — Philip Tetlock
- _The Signal and the Noise_ — Nate Silver
- RAND Corporation methodology papers
