# Economics & Market Mental Models

Mental models from economics that explain how markets, incentives, and resource allocation work.

| #   | Framework                                  | Description                                                                                                                                                                          | Origin                       |
| --- | ------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------- |
| 142 | **Supply and Demand**                      | Price is determined by the intersection of supply (producers) and demand (consumers). Shifts in either curve change equilibrium price and quantity.                                  | Adam Smith; Alfred Marshall  |
| 143 | **Opportunity Cost**                       | The true cost of any choice is what you give up by not choosing the next-best alternative. Invisible costs are still real costs.                                                     | Friedrich von Wieser         |
| 144 | **Incentives**                             | People respond to incentives. Get the incentives right and behavior follows. Misaligned incentives produce perverse outcomes. "Show me the incentive and I'll show you the outcome." | Economics; Charlie Munger    |
| 145 | **Marginal Utility / Diminishing Returns** | The additional benefit from one more unit of something decreases as you get more of it. Applies to consumption, investment, and effort.                                              | William Stanley Jevons       |
| 146 | **Comparative Advantage**                  | Even if one party is better at everything, both benefit from trade when each specializes in what they produce at the lowest relative cost.                                           | David Ricardo                |
| 147 | **Agency Problem (Principal-Agent)**       | When agents (managers) have different incentives than principals (owners), they may act in self-interest rather than the principal's interest.                                       | Jensen & Meckling            |
| 148 | **Moral Hazard**                           | When insulated from risk (e.g., by insurance or bailouts), people take greater risks than they otherwise would.                                                                      | Adam Smith; insurance theory |
| 149 | **Information Asymmetry**                  | When one party in a transaction has more/better information than the other. Leads to adverse selection and market failures.                                                          | George Akerlof               |
| 150 | **Game Theory**                            | The study of strategic interactions where outcomes depend on others' actions. Key concepts: Nash equilibrium, prisoner's dilemma, zero-sum vs. positive-sum.                         | John von Neumann, John Nash  |
| 151 | **Creative Destruction**                   | Capitalism advances through the destruction of old industries by new ones. Essential for long-term progress, painful in the short term.                                              | Joseph Schumpeter            |
| 152 | **Efficient Market Hypothesis**            | Asset prices fully reflect all available information. Implies it's impossible to consistently beat the market.                                                                       | Eugene Fama                  |
| 153 | **Gresham's Law**                          | "Bad money drives out good." When undervalued and overvalued items circulate together, the overvalued (inferior) ones dominate.                                                      | Sir Thomas Gresham           |
| 154 | **Mr. Market**                             | Imagine the stock market as an emotional partner who offers wildly varying prices every day. Your job: take advantage of his irrationality, don't be infected by it.                 | Benjamin Graham              |

## Key Sources

- _The Wealth of Nations_ — Adam Smith
- _The Intelligent Investor_ — Benjamin Graham
- _Capitalism, Socialism and Democracy_ — Joseph Schumpeter
- _The Market for Lemons_ — George Akerlof
- _Poor Charlie's Almanack_ — Charles Munger
