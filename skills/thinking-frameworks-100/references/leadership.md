# Leadership & Organizational Frameworks

Frameworks for leading people, managing change, and building high-performing organizations.

| #   | Framework                                 | Description                                                                                                                                                               | Origin                    |
| --- | ----------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| 155 | **Servant Leadership**                    | The leader's primary role is to serve — empower, develop, and remove obstacles for their team. Authority comes from service, not position.                                | Robert Greenleaf          |
| 156 | **Situational Leadership**                | Adjust your leadership style (directing, coaching, supporting, delegating) based on the competence and commitment level of each team member.                              | Hersey & Blanchard        |
| 157 | **Radical Candor**                        | Effective feedback requires both caring personally AND challenging directly. 2×2 matrix: Radical Candor, Obnoxious Aggression, Ruinous Empathy, Manipulative Insincerity. | Kim Scott                 |
| 158 | **SCARF Model**                           | Engagement is driven by five domains: Status, Certainty, Autonomy, Relatedness, and Fairness. Threats to any domain trigger a "fight or flight" response.                 | David Rock                |
| 159 | **Kotter's 8-Step Change Model**          | Create urgency → Build coalition → Form vision → Communicate → Empower action → Generate wins → Consolidate → Anchor in culture.                                          | John Kotter               |
| 160 | **ADKAR Model**                           | Change requires: Awareness (of need), Desire (to participate), Knowledge (how to change), Ability (skills to implement), Reinforcement (to sustain).                      | Jeffrey Hiatt (Prosci)    |
| 161 | **Directly Responsible Individual (DRI)** | For every project or decision, one person is clearly designated as the decision-maker and accountable party. Eliminates ambiguity.                                        | Apple (Steve Jobs legacy) |
| 162 | **Psychological Safety**                  | Team members feel safe to take interpersonal risks: speak up, admit mistakes, ask questions, disagree — without fear of punishment or humiliation.                        | Amy Edmondson             |
| 163 | **Tuckman's Stages of Group Development** | Teams progress through: Forming → Storming → Norming → Performing (→ Adjourning). Understanding the stage helps leaders intervene appropriately.                          | Bruce Tuckman             |
| 164 | **Retrospective (Agile)**                 | After each sprint/iteration, the team reflects: What went well? What didn't? What will we change? Embeds continuous improvement in the work rhythm.                       | Agile/Scrum methodology   |
