# Mathematical & Engineering Models

Mental models from math, physics, and engineering with broad applicability.

| #   | Framework                                  | Description                                                                                                                                                                 | Origin                       |
| --- | ------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| 165 | **Compounding**                            | Small, consistent gains accumulate exponentially over time. The most powerful force in finance, learning, and habit building.                                               | Mathematics / finance        |
| 166 | **Power Laws**                             | In many systems, a small number of items account for a disproportionate share of the effect. Unlike normal distributions, extremes dominate.                                | Vilfredo Pareto; physics     |
| 167 | **Normal Distribution (Bell Curve)**       | Many natural phenomena cluster around a mean with predictable standard deviations. But beware: not everything is normally distributed (see Power Laws).                     | Carl Friedrich Gauss         |
| 168 | **Margin of Safety**                       | Build buffers. Engineers overdesign bridges (safety factor), investors buy below intrinsic value. The buffer between your estimate and disaster.                            | Engineering; Benjamin Graham |
| 169 | **Redundancy**                             | Critical systems include backup components so that failure of one part doesn't bring down the whole. Applies to engineering, organizations, and personal planning.          | Engineering; Nassim Taleb    |
| 170 | **Critical Mass**                          | The minimum input needed to trigger a self-sustaining chain reaction. Applies to nuclear physics, social movements, viral products, and any threshold-dependent phenomenon. | Nuclear physics              |
| 171 | **Entropy (Second Law of Thermodynamics)** | Left alone, systems tend toward disorder. Maintenance, energy, and effort are required to maintain (or increase) order. Applies to organizations, habits, relationships.    | Rudolf Clausius              |
| 172 | **Activation Energy**                      | The initial energy required to start a reaction. In human terms: the effort needed to start a habit, project, or change. Reduce it to make desired actions easier.          | Svante Arrhenius; chemistry  |
| 173 | **Leverage**                               | A small input force applied at the right point produces a disproportionately large output. Archimedes' principle applies to business, relationships, learning.              | Archimedes                   |
