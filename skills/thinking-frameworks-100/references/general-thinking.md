# General Thinking Concepts

Foundational, cross-disciplinary frameworks that improve the quality of reasoning itself.

| #   | Framework                        | Description                                                                                                                      | Origin                              |
| --- | -------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| 1   | **First Principles Thinking**    | Decompose a problem down to its fundamental truths, then reason up from there — stripping away assumptions and analogies.        | Aristotle; popularized by Elon Musk |
| 2   | **Second-Order Thinking**        | Consider not just the immediate consequences of a decision but the consequences of the consequences (and beyond).                | Howard Marks, Shane Parrish         |
| 3   | **Inversion**                    | Instead of asking "How do I succeed?", ask "How could I guarantee failure?" — then avoid those things. Reveals hidden solutions. | Carl Jacobi; Charlie Munger         |
| 4   | **The Map is Not the Territory** | All models, maps, and representations are simplifications of reality. Never confuse your model with reality itself.              | Alfred Korzybski                    |
| 5   | **Circle of Competence**         | Know what you know and what you don't. Operate within areas of genuine expertise. Expand your circle deliberately.               | Warren Buffett, Charlie Munger      |
| 6   | **Occam's Razor**                | Among competing hypotheses, the one with the fewest assumptions should be selected. Prefer simplicity over complexity.           | William of Ockham (14th c.)         |
| 7   | **Hanlon's Razor**               | Never attribute to malice that which is adequately explained by ignorance or incompetence.                                       | Robert J. Hanlon                    |
| 8   | **Probabilistic Thinking**       | Replace binary (yes/no) thinking with probability estimates. Use Bayesian updating to refine beliefs as new evidence arrives.    | Thomas Bayes; Nate Silver           |
| 9   | **Thought Experiments**          | Mentally simulate scenarios to explore consequences without real-world risk. Used to test hypotheses and reveal contradictions.  | Einstein, Galileo, Schrödinger      |
| 10  | **Munger's Latticework**         | Build an interconnected web of mental models from multiple disciplines. Their combined power exceeds any single model.           | Charlie Munger                      |
| 11  | **Falsification**                | Actively seek evidence that could disprove your theory rather than confirm it. The hallmark of scientific rigor.                 | Karl Popper                         |
| 12  | **Via Negativa**                 | Improve by subtracting (removing the harmful) rather than adding. Often more robust than additive approaches.                    | Nassim Taleb                        |
