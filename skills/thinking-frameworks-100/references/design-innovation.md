# Design & Innovation

Frameworks for user-centered problem solving, prototyping, and innovation management.

| #   | Framework                        | Description                                                                                                                                                                 | Origin                         |
| --- | -------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| 130 | **Design Thinking (IDEO)**       | A human-centered process: Empathize → Define → Ideate → Prototype → Test. Iterative, collaborative, and grounded in user needs.                                             | IDEO; David Kelley, Tim Brown  |
| 131 | **Double Diamond**               | Diverge (Discover) → Converge (Define) → Diverge (Develop) → Converge (Deliver). Alternates between exploratory and focused thinking in two phases.                         | UK Design Council              |
| 132 | **Lean Startup**                 | Build → Measure → Learn. Launch a minimum viable product (MVP), test hypotheses with real customers, pivot or persevere based on validated learning.                        | Eric Ries                      |
| 133 | **Minimum Viable Product (MVP)** | The simplest version of a product that allows you to test your core hypothesis with real users and gather maximum validated learning with minimum effort.                   | Eric Ries; Frank Robinson      |
| 134 | **Design Sprint**                | A 5-day process for testing ideas: Map → Sketch → Decide → Prototype → Test with real users. Compresses months of work into one week.                                       | Jake Knapp (Google Ventures)   |
| 135 | **Ten Types of Innovation**      | Innovation beyond product: Profit Model, Network, Structure, Process, Product Performance, Product System, Service, Channel, Brand, Customer Engagement.                    | Doblin / Deloitte              |
| 136 | **Kano Model**                   | Classify product features by customer satisfaction: Must-have (expected), Performance (more is better), Delight (unexpected), and Indifferent. Prioritize delight features. | Noriaki Kano                   |
| 137 | **Stage-Gate Process**           | Innovation projects pass through sequential gates: Scoping → Business Case → Development → Testing → Launch. Each gate requires meeting criteria.                           | Robert Cooper                  |
| 138 | **Value Proposition Canvas**     | Map Customer Jobs, Pains, and Gains against your product's Pain Relievers, Gain Creators, and Products/Services. Ensures product-market fit.                                | Alexander Osterwalder          |
| 139 | **Disruptive Innovation**        | New entrants with simpler, cheaper offerings disrupt incumbents from below. Incumbents overlook the threat because it initially serves "low-end" or non-consumers.          | Clayton Christensen            |
| 140 | **Human-Centered Design**        | Deeply understand the people you're designing for through immersive research, then co-create and iterate solutions with them. Prioritizes empathy and inclusion.            | IDEO.org                       |
| 141 | **Experience-Based Co-Design**   | Engage clinicians, patients, and staff as equal design partners to redesign services based on real experiences — not just outcomes data.                                    | NHS / Paul Bate & Robert Glenn |
